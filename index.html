The Story

A life built
from the hardest
chapters.

"For thirty years, I was told it was in my head. It wasn't. And now I help others find what took me decades to learn: that they deserve to be believed."

I am a certified Mental Health Coach (Abide Network, Level 2), a psychology student at Liberty University, a direct response copywriter, a community director, a homeschooling mother, a wife to Dave — and a person who spent three decades fighting to be believed about her own body.

In 2024, I was formally diagnosed with Central Sensitization Syndrome — a neurological condition that rewires how the brain processes pain. After completing the Mayo Clinic Pain Rehabilitation Center program in April 2025, I walked away with something I'd never had before: a language for what my nervous system had been doing all along. And a mission to share it.

My work sits at the intersection of neuroscience, faith, and lived experience. I specialize in neurodivergent individuals and complex health situations — the people medicine has often left behind.

Through my Sophrosyne Method — a coaching framework drawing on nervous system literacy, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, and faith integration — I companion clients into lives that are bigger than their diagnoses. I also serve as a patient advocate, including over two years of intensive advocacy work navigating international healthcare systems on behalf of clients who cannot navigate them alone.

I believe the most credentialed thing about me is not a certificate. It is the fact that I have lived this — and survived it with curiosity still intact.

What I Do

Six roles.
One throughline: people.

01 —

Mental Health Coach

Certified Level 2 coach (Abide Network) specializing in neurodivergent individuals, complex chronic health conditions, and faith-integrated healing. Creator of the Sophrosyne Method — a framework grounded in nervous system literacy and ACT principles.

02 —

Patient & Systems Advocate

Over two years of intensive advocacy navigating international healthcare systems, welfare programs, and psychiatric evaluation pipelines — producing clinical documentation packages, translating foreign medical records, and filing formal complaints on behalf of clients who cannot advocate for themselves.

03 —

Direct Response Copywriter

Specializing in ministry and faith-based organizations. Long-form sales pages, email sequences, event campaigns, and social content that converts — for organizations like Abide Network. Known for the "Agent 2" approach: copy that feels human because a human wrote it.

04 —

Community Director

Co-founder and Director of One Accord: The Guild — a Discord-based faith and gaming community of 875+ members built around discipleship, not performance. A space where people belong on purpose.

05 —

Psychology Student & Researcher

Currently completing a B.S. in Psychology at Liberty University. Deep academic and personal study in CSS, the HPA/SAM axes, NMDA receptor function, TBI history, and the neurological underpinnings of chronic pain — treated not only as curriculum, but as a calling.

06 —

Public Voice & Writer

Sharing the unvarnished journey of chronic illness, neurodivergence, advocacy, and recovery — publicly, unapologetically, and with the precision of someone who understands the neuroscience behind their own story. An anti-stigma voice in a space that needs it.
